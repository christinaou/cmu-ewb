HISTORY

On the morning of August 31st, 2012, 45 CMU students, including two Stever Resident Assistants, the president of the CMU Chapter of Engineers Without Borders, and many members of the class of 2016, woke up early and took the time to make a difference in their local community. Their goal? Help contribute to the development of Emerald Park, a restoration project which will connect 257 acres of forested park land to create a 19 mile trail plan.

We worked with three members of the Mount Washington Community Development Corporation on three different areas of the up-and-coming Emerald Park. One group cleared weeds from an area that will become a trail-head, another cleaned trash from a dumpsite that will eventually be a part of the park, while a third group cleared vines and debris from a trail under construction.

The amount and variety of trash we collected proved that our work was worthwhile. This project is ongoing and CMU Engineers Without Borders plans to organize more of these service trips in the future, so that we can continue to make an effort toward adding green space to Pittsburgh.
